Who are we?

We are a cutting-edge mobile application designed to predict stock market prices and ensure secure, reliable trading environments.

What we do?

We offer a comprehensive suite of features aimed at predicting stock prices, identifying anomalies, assessing system suitability for predictions, and detecting fraudulent activities in trading.

What are the key features?

Our app leverages advanced algorithms to predict stock market trends and prices, ensuring traders are well-informed. It includes anomaly detection to identify irregularities within the application, system, network, and device, guaranteeing optimal functionality. Additionally, it conducts system suitability checks to ensure that all components meet the necessary criteria for accurate stock predictions. Lastly, it provides fraud detection by monitoring and reporting suspicious activities within companies, safeguarding against trading fraud.

Why we do it?

To empower traders with reliable predictions and secure trading experiences, ensuring confidence and trust in the stock market.

How we do it?

We provide a mobile application that combines sophisticated algorithms with real-time data analysis, accessible to traders anywhere, anytime.

Since when?

Application will be live from 31st of May 2024.

Research problem

The research problem focuses on enhancing efficiency, reliability, and user-centricity in stock market prediction and fraud detection. It aims to integrate machine learning algorithms for dynamic market data adaptation, improving prediction accuracy and timeliness. Emphasizing user-centric design, the research seeks to develop personalized prediction systems that cater to individual investor preferences and risk profiles. A comprehensive comparative analysis of prediction models, including traditional, time series, and hybrid approaches, is conducted to determine their effectiveness across various market conditions. The research prioritizes interpretability and explainability in prediction models to foster user trust and facilitate model validation. Additionally, it addresses market manipulation and fraudulent activities in the Colombo stock market by developing sophisticated, adaptable fraud detection systems, promoting transparency and fairness. Overall, the research adopts a holistic approach to advance stock market prediction and fraud detection, emphasizing user trust, transparency, and market integrity.

Research Gap

The development of a mobile application for predicting stock market prices, detecting anomalies, assessing system suitability, and identifying trading fraud represents a substantial technological advancement. This application has the potential to revolutionize how investors and traders interact with the stock market by providing real-time insights and robust security measures. However, to fully realize this potential, it is crucial to address several research gaps within each component of the application. This discussion will outline the primary research gaps in stock market price prediction, anomaly detection, system suitability checks, and fraud detection in trading.

1. Personalized Stock market price prediction system

Gap : Despite advancements in machine learning and personalized stock market prediction systems, several critical research gaps persist. Comparative studies systematically evaluating various machine learning algorithms for prediction accuracy, scalability, and usability are lacking, as existing literature often focuses on specific algorithms or methodologies in isolation. Additionally, many personalized prediction systems fail to adequately incorporate user preferences, risk tolerance, and investment goals, necessitating research into effective methods for integrating user-specific data into predictive models. Transparency and interpretability remain significant challenges, with investors hesitant to rely on black-box models without understanding the underlying factors driving predictions. This underscores the need for research that enhances model interpretability to build user trust. Furthermore, the dynamic nature of financial markets requires prediction systems that can adapt to rapid changes in real-time. Research focused on continuously updating and refining predictive models based on evolving market dynamics is essential to maintain their relevance and effectiveness. Addressing these gaps will advance personalized stock market prediction systems, enabling i nvestors to make more informed and confident decisions in dynamic financial markets.

2. Improve personalized stock recommendations with fraud detection

Gap : The existing literature on fraud detection in financial markets predominantly focuses on either non-time series models, such as XGBoost and Random Forest, or time series models like ARIMA, in isolation. This fragmented approach overlooks the potential benefits of integrating both model types. Non-time series models excel at detecting anomalies in historical data by capturing complex relationships, while time series models are proficient in forecasting and identifying temporal patterns. However, relying solely on one type of model limits the detection capabilities—non-time series models may miss subtle temporal manipulations, and time series models may overlook immediate anomalies. The research gap lies in the lack of a comprehensive approach that synergizes these methodologies. An integrated model combining XGBoost, Random Forest, and ARIMA could leverage the strengths of both approaches, o ffering a holistic analysis of market data and enhancing fraud detection accuracy. This integration not only addresses the limitations of individual models but also improves the reliability of personalized stock recommendation systems, providing investors with more accurate and tailored insights, thereby enhancing trust and decision-making in the financial market ecosystem.

3. Utilize Image Processing Techniques for Stock market chart analysis to enable Visual pattern recognition

Gap : Although significant progress has been made in integrating UI/UX design with machine learning in prediction applications, several research gaps remain. One critical gap is the limited focus on anomaly detection in application performance metrics specific to stock market prediction applications. While the use of machine learning for stock price prediction and fraud detection is well-documented, its application in monitoring performance metrics such as CPU usage, memory usage, and network traffic is relatively unexplored. Additionally, the impact of UI/UX design on user engagement is insufficiently evaluated. Despite the acknowledged importance of design principles, empirical studies measuring how design elements influence user engagement and satisfaction in financial applications are scarce. Moreover, there is a lack of comparative analysis of anomaly detection algorithms like isolation forest and one-class SVM for monitoring application performance. Current research tends to focus on individual algorithms without systematically comparing their effectiveness in terms of accuracy, efficiency, and scalability. Addressing these gaps will enhance the reliability, usability, and performance monitoring of stock market prediction applications, leading to better decision-making for users.

While mobile application represents a significant step forward in financial technology, addressing the identified research gaps is essential for enhancing its functionality, accuracy, and reliability. By focusing on these areas, future research can provide more robust and secure tools for investors and traders, ultimately leading to more informed and safer financial decision-making. Addressing these gaps will not only improve the current application but also pave the way for further innovations in financial predictive technologies.

Methodologies



Improve personalized stock recommendations with fraud detection :

The methodology involved consultations with stock market professionals to inform the selection of machine learning algorithms and feature engineering techniques aimed at enhancing personalized stock recommendations by detecting market manipulation. Historical trading data from the Colombo Stock Exchange were meticulously collected, preprocessed, and combined using pandas, enabling comprehensive analysis across multiple securities. Three machine learning models—Random Forest, XGBoost, and Autoregressive Integrated Moving Average (ARIMA)—were developed and trained using historical data to detect market anomalies. Evaluation metrics including accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score were employed to assess model performance, revealing the effectiveness of XGBoost and Random Forest in capturing complex market dynamics, while ARIMA exhibited moderate performance, underscoring the importance of exploring diverse modeling approaches in financial analysis.

Personalized Stock market price prediction system :

This study develops a robust personalized stock market price prediction system using historical data from Investing.com and Colombo Stock Exchange. The dataset includes opening and closing prices, high and low prices, and trading volume. Feature selection emphasizes essential market dynamics, excluding sentiment analysis and macroeconomic indicators. Model selection involves Random Forest, SVM, XGBoost, ARIMA, and LSTM, with hyperparameter tuning. The training and evaluation process incorporates sliding windows and overlapping sequences for temporal dependency. Online learning techniques adapt models to real-time market changes. Evaluation metrics include Mean Absolute Error (MAE), Root Mean Square Error (RMSE), and precision-recall curves. Cloud integration ensures scalability, storing model results and contextual information securely. Continuous improvement involves regular updates, feature engineering, and adaptation to emerging market trends. Feedback loops capture user interactions for refining predictions based on evolving market dynamics. This streamlined methodology provides a comprehensive and adaptive approach to personalized stock market price prediction.

Utilize Image Processing Techniques for Stock market chart analysis to enable Visual pattern recognition:

In the initial phase of the research, collaborative efforts with industry experts enriched the understanding of stock market intricacies. Through insightful discussions, visual patterns signaling potential fraud in stock market charts were identified. Simultaneously, datasets for individual companies were meticulously obtained from reputable financial sources to ensure diversity in market conditions and historical dynamics. Transitioning to the second phase, acquired datasets underwent thorough integration using the Pandas library, incorporating preprocessing steps for consistency. Guided by insights from expert consultations, an iterative fine-tuning process adjusted dataset parameters to align with identified visual patterns of potential fraud. The subsequent training and evaluation of the DenseNet121 model on the fine-tuned dataset employed a comprehensive set of metrics to gauge its efficacy in detecting fraudulent activities within the stock market. This methodological approach, shaped by expert input and refined through iterative adjustments, establishes a robust framework for the effective application of image processing techniques in stock market fraud detection.

Optimizing Battery Efficiency in Stock market Prediction Applications:-
A comprehensive approach thrrough UI/UX design, network optimization and machine learning-based app performance monitoring:

The methodology for enhancing the stock market prediction application encompasses various key areas. Firstly, in UI/UX Design Improvements, a focus is placed on identifying power-intensive UI elements, optimizing visual elements, streamlining interactive elements, and conducting user testing to align changes with user preferences. Secondly, Network Optimization Strategies involve minimizing unnecessary network requests, using efficient data transfer protocols like JSON, implementing caching mechanisms, optimizing image and multimedia handling, and continuous monitoring of network usage. Thirdly, in Machine Learning Model Development and Integration, suitable ML algorithms are selected, diverse training datasets are prepared, models are trained and integrated for real-time monitoring. Lastly, Dataset and Evaluation Metrics involve comprehensive data collection, labeling for ML training, evaluation using standard metrics like precision and recall, and gathering user feedback on performance improvements. This holistic approach aims to optimize battery usage through UI/UX enhancements, network efficiency improvements, and the integration of a machine learning model for real-time performance monitoring while ensuring user-centric design through continuous testing and feedback loops.
